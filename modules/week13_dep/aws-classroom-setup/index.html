Instructor Notes
AWS Classroom Setup

The final project will be deployed using AWS ECS (Elastic Container Service). The simplest way to get our students setup with access to free AWS Educate - Student (opens new window) accounts is using AWS Classrooms. As a prerequisite, you as a Professor will need to register with AWS Educate as an educator (opens new window). Algonquin College is already registered with AWS as a higher education institution.

# Request a classroom

  1. Once your account is registered, login to the AWS Educate Portal (opens new window).
  2. Choose "Classrooms & Credits" from the top navigation.
  3. Click on the card labeled "Request or got to an AWS Educate Classroom".
  4. Scroll down to select a template. We will use the "Starter Account Template".
  5. Click "Next" at the bottom of the screen.
  6. Enter classroom details.
  7. Download the email list Excel template. Populate the first column with student email addresses and upload.
  8. The status of your request can be seen on the "My Classrooms" page.
  9. After about 24 hours, the request should be approved an your status page will look like this.
  10. Click on the green "Activate" button, and then confirm in the modal dialog. This will allow the students to access this classroom's resources.
  11. Click the blue "Go to Classroom" button.
  12. Agree to the sneaky last minute third-party terms and conditions. The short version is (and I am paraphrasing) you are an adult and you will not take any intentional actions to hack or otherwise impair the service.
  13. This dashboard will let you monitor which services students are using and how quickly they are consuming their $50 classroom credits.

NOTE: Classroom credits are separate and in addition to the $100 credit that students will receive for their AWS Educate Starter Account.

# Student View

If you click on the "home" icon in the Vocareum main navigation, you will have a option to enter your classroom as a student.

Click through "as a student" and you will see a screen with the student's classroom credit summary. There are some FAQs on the left, but the main target is the "AWS Console" button.

TIP

Yes, you as the teacher also get an additional $50 classroom credit. This will let you mirror your students exercises without depleting your primary Educator Account credits.

Click the "AWS Console" button. It will try to open a pop-up tab, which the browser will likely block by default. Click the "blocked pop-up" icon to allow access.

You should then see a new tab pre-logged in to the AWS console.

NOTE: The AWS region defaults to N. Virginia (us-east-1).
I believe that all classroom resources must run in this region.
